The open question.

Guru has been shipping US-built custom software for twenty years. The site says it plainly: "Your Tech Team, Ready Now." Fifty engineers, two hundred projects, anchor accounts like CrewTracks and the Utah Transit Authority. The AI services page went live recently, HIMSS 2026 is on the calendar, and eMerge Americas is the East-Coast and LatAm signal. The base business is steady. The AI layer is the open question.

The open question is not whether Guru can build AI features. The bench can. It is how to price, scope, and stage the work so the AI engagements carry the same margin discipline as the rest of the book. Most AI requests collapse into three shapes: a database problem wearing a model's clothing, a workflow that needs rule-based logic first, and a small slice of genuine AI.

60% Traditional code and database.
30% Rule-based logic.
10% Genuine AI.

Call it computational orchestration. Deciding which layer each piece of a problem belongs on, before a single engineer gets assigned.

That framework is the thing Guru's team is currently quoting around rather than with.

What the engagement looks like.

For a firm with Guru's shape, it looks like this. A two-day internal workshop with engineering leads and the client-facing team. Real client scopes get rebuilt against the 60/30/10 frame. The output is a repeatable diagnostic your team can run with clients, an internal scoping checklist for your architects, and a context architecture that survives staff rotation. A working artifact your bench will actually use.

The closest proof.

A scoped sprint with the CTO team at Feeld, a product company with its own engineers trying to operationalize AI inside an existing stack. The deliverables were a workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. The shape maps cleanly onto a services firm. A framework your team internalizes, not a system you hand off.

The methodology.

The paper is Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture, submitted to ACM TiiS. Repo here: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-. It is the scaffold that lets any engineer on your bench pick up an AI engagement without rebuilding the architecture from scratch.

Where the work routes.

When a client engagement stretches into production ML pipeline, data warehouse modernization, or full-stack build below the orchestration layer, Eduba partners with NLP Logix, which has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists. That gives Guru a clean referral pattern without conceding the primary relationship.
